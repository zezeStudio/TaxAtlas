The PAYE System

In the UK, most employees pay tax through **Pay As You Earn (PAYE)**. Your employer deducts income tax and National Insurance before paying your wages.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Tax Code? expand_more
Your tax code (e.g., 1257L) tells your employer how much tax-free income you’re entitled to. "L" means you're entitled to the standard tax-free Personal Allowance.
Do I need to file a Tax Return? expand_more
If you're only an employee, usually no. However, you must file a Self Assessment if you earn over £150,000, are self-employed, or have other untaxed income.
How does Gift Aid work? expand_more
Donating to charity through Gift Aid allows the charity to claim back 25p for every £1 you give. Higher-rate taxpayers can also claim back the difference.
